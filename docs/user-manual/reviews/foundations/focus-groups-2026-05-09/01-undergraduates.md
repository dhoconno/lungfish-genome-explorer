# Foundations focus group 1: undergraduates

**Date:** 2026-05-09
**Method:** Five undergraduate biology personas reading the eight foundations chapters as a unit and reporting honest reactions. See `synthesis.md` in this directory for the cross-cutting issues this group raised.

The undergraduate group flagged the following issues most prominently. The synthesis document combines findings from this group with the PhD, early-career, and power-user groups.

## Key findings from this group

- Chapters 6, 7, 8 do not feel like foundations. Four of five undergraduate personas independently flagged that chapters 1-5 read as a tight unit teaching viral-genomics file formats and concepts; chapters 6-8 read as a separate "Getting Started with the App" unit and break the conceptual flow.
- "Amplicon" is the single most-stumbled word. Three of five undergrad personas hit it as unglossed. (Note: the term is now defined in F03 with a one-line gloss at first use.)
- The CIGAR string `5S140M5S` example is a stumbling point. Three of five undergrads flagged this; one would have closed the page at it.
- "Soft-clip" is used in F03 (amplicon) and F04 (alignment) before the canonical definition lands.
- ASCII offset 33 explanation in F02 makes no sense to readers who do not know what ASCII is.
- The 1-based vs 0-based discussion appears in three chapters and feels redundant by chapter 5.

## What worked

- Chapter 5's two-row PASS/ft contrast worked as a competence builder. Four of five undergrads felt confident reading a VCF afterward.
- Chapter 5's haploid AF section. All personas praised the framing.
- The variant-notation breakdown in Chapter 1 (`MN908947.3:21618 C>T`) is a competence checkpoint they passed.
- Concrete numbers (86,281 read pairs, ~5 minutes wall time, 250 MB) ground every chapter.
