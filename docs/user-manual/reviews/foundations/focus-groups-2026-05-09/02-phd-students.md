# Foundations focus group 2: PhD students

**Date:** 2026-05-09
**Method:** Five PhD-student personas reading the eight foundations chapters as a unit. See `synthesis.md` for the cross-cutting issues this group raised.

## Key findings from this group

- Scope statement missing or buried. PhD personas reading early chapters wanted an upfront "this is for viral genomics" sentence in chapter 1; bacterial WGS, eukaryotic, and human-disease-allele readers waste time before learning their workflow is out of scope.
- LoFreq's "permissive defaults" claim is misattributed in Foundations. LoFreq is conservative on per-site evidence; the default-AF term needs disambiguation.
- iVar QUAL/FILTER attribution is loose. The chapter currently treats Lungfish's converter behavior as native iVar behavior.
- Foundations chapters cite no upstream tool papers (Quick 2017 ARTIC, Grubaugh 2019 iVar, Wilm 2012 LoFreq, Li 2018 minimap2, Ewing 1998 Phred).
- The codon-merging behavior is ambiguous about agency (iVar vs Lungfish converter).

## What worked

- The haploid AF chapter is universally praised. All PhD personas including the human-genetics persona cited it as the strongest piece of writing.
- Chapter 1's reference-coordinate framing maps cleanly onto GATK/genomics thinking.
- The provenance sidecar story is best-in-class.
